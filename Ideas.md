\section{Results}

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}

To rigorously evaluate the efficacy of our disentangled training framework in reducing sycophantic behavior, we adopt a suite of quantitative metrics that capture both pressure-induced deviation and context sensitivity. These metrics extend prior work on sycophancy measurement by explicitly decomposing model responses along authority and evidence axes, enabling fine-grained evaluation of sycophancy removal.:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

\textbf{Pressure Sycophancy Score (PSS).}  
The Pressure Sycophancy Score quantifies the extent to which a model’s response deviates from its baseline (no pressure) output when exposed to increasingly authoritative prompts. Formally, for each seed pair $(C,Q)$ and pressure level $P_j$, let $r_j(C,Q)$ denote the model’s structured response and $r_\emptyset(C,Q)$ denote the baseline. The PSS aggregates a distance measure (e.g., NLI entailment score difference) across pressure levels:
\[
\text{PSS} = \frac{1}{|J|} \sum_{j} \Delta(r_\emptyset, r_j),
\]
where $\Delta(\cdot,\cdot)$ is an entailment-based distance function. A lower PSS indicates stronger invariance to pressure cues and thus reduced sycophancy.  
\textit{Intuition:} PSS isolates the effect of authority cues on model outputs independent of context fidelity.

\textbf{Context Faithfulness Score (CFS).}  
The Context Faithfulness Score measures whether a model’s outputs properly reflect differences between paired contexts $C$ and $C'$ while controlling for pressure. For each context pair, we define:
\[
\text{CFS} = \frac{1}{2}(\Delta(r_\emptyset(C,Q),r_\emptyset(C',Q)) + \Delta(r_j(C,Q),r_j(C',Q))),
\]
averaging over all pressure levels $j$. A high CFS indicates that the model distinguishes factual evidence from its inverse across contexts.  
\textit{Intuition:} CFS captures sensitivity to relevant evidence regardless of pressure cues.

\textbf{Pressure-Amplified Context Flip (PACF).}  
PACF evaluates whether exposure to increasing authority amplifies context blindness — i.e., the collapse of distinction between $C$ and $C'$. It is defined as the difference in context discrimination between no pressure and high pressure conditions:
\[
\text{PACF} = \text{CFS}_{\emptyset} - \text{CFS}_{\text{high}},
\]
where $\text{CFS}_{\text{high}}$ indicates the score at the highest pressure level. A low or negative PACF suggests minimal degradation of evidence discrimination under pressure.  
\textit{Intuition:} PACF detects whether authority cues systematically override context fidelity.

\textbf{Generic Agreement Score (GAS).}  
The Generic Agreement Score captures whether a model’s outputs are uniformly agreeable across contexts and pressures, regardless of evidence. GAS is defined by the inverse of output diversity across all grid cells for a given seed:
\[
\text{GAS} = 1 - \frac{1}{|G|} \sum_{(P,C)} \text{Dist}(r(P,C), \bar{r}),
\]
where $\bar{r}$ is the centroid response representation and Dist is a semantic distance. A high GAS reflects generic compliance rather than evidence-responsive generation.  
\textit{Intuition:} GAS diagnoses degenerate uniform agreement and failure to discriminate evidence or pressure signals.

\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Dataset}

We construct a benchmark of \textbf{395 topic groups} spanning four domains
(\emph{mathematics}, \emph{physics}, \emph{opinion}, and \emph{political science}),
designed to elicit sycophantic responses under controlled social-pressure contexts.
Each group is anchored to a distinct topic-question pair and contains a
\emph{baseline response} to the unpressured query, alongside six pressured
variants generated by crossing three pressure levels
(\texttt{low}, \texttt{medium}, \texttt{high}) with two context orientations
(\texttt{original}, \texttt{opposite}).
The \texttt{opposite} context encodes a logically negated opinion relative to the
seed, enabling direct measurement of position-consistency under adversarial pressure.

\subsection{Baselines and Evaluation Setup}

We evaluate each model by generating responses to all pressured prompts and computing
the four metrics defined in Section~\ref{sec:metrics}.
Models are queried with a standardised system prompt instructing them not to let
authority or credentials influence their answers.
For proprietary models we use greedy decoding via the respective APIs;
for open-weight models we use 4-bit quantised inference with a fixed seed.
All scores are averaged over both context orientations and all three pressure
levels unless stated otherwise.

\subsection{Main Results}
\label{sec:main_results}

Tables~\ref{tab:factual_results} and~\ref{tab:subjective_results} report metric
scores across models on tshe \emph{factual} (mathematics + physics) and
\emph{opinion} (opinion + political) subsets respectively.
Lower PSS and GAS and higher CFS and PACF indicate better resistance to
sycophantic pressure.
We observe a consistent trend: reasoning-specialised models
(e.g.\ DeepSeek-R1, Qwen3) show lower PSS on factual tasks but can exhibit
elevated GAS on subjective prompts, suggesting distinct failure modes across
domain types.
Larger models do not uniformly outperform smaller ones, consistent with prior
findings that sycophancy is not simply a capacity deficit~\citep{sharma2023towards}.

\subsection{GRPO Training Results}
\label{sec:grpo_results}

We fine-tune Llama-3.1 (8B) using the two-phase pipeline described in
Section~\ref{sec:method}: a supervised fine-tuning (SFT) phase on unpressured
baseline responses, followed by GRPO with our multi-component reward.
Table~\ref{tab:grpo_results} reports metric evolution across training stages.

\textbf{GRPO v1} (na\"ive reward without length constraints) collapsed to
short vague completions by epoch~0.8, driving KL divergence above~0.74
and producing catastrophic position-consistency regression
($\mathrm{PACF} = {-0.42}$).
\textbf{GRPO v2} introduced a hard length floor (\texttt{min\_new\_tokens}$=$80),
ideal-length incentive, and strengthened KL penalty ($\beta=0.2$), recovering
stable training and improving PSS and CFS but leaving GAS unreduced and
PACF below the pre-training baseline.
\textbf{GRPO v3} (this work) adds an explicit position-consistency reward
$R_{\mathrm{pos}}$ ($\varepsilon=0.4$), doubles the generic-agreement penalty
($\delta=0.6$), raises \texttt{min\_new\_tokens} to~100, and trains for two
epochs with cosine learning-rate decay and 10\% warmup.
Results marked \textdagger{} are pending completion of the v3 run.

\paragraph{Reward component ablation.}
Table~\ref{tab:reward_ablation} measures the marginal contribution of each

to revert toward the pre-training level, validating our motivation for
the doubled penalty in v3.
